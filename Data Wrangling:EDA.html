What is in your data?

The data set from data.gov contains information on a summary of statistics of inbound crossings at the US Canada and US Mexico border at port level. This is categorized through information such as Data, Port Name, Border, Measure (ie. whether it is a personal vehicle, truck, bus, and so on). This provides some insight as to what are common ports, common types of transportation, providing a possible trend/pattern.

How will these data be useful for studying the phenomenon you're interested in?

Since this data set provides border crossings over the years, it can be analyzed to gauge potential patterns in seasonal increases in traffic. If there is an understanding of this trend, then it may be useful in predicting border corssing volumes based on this, as well as having the potential to go beyond to other factors.

What are the challenges you've resolved or expect to face in using them?

Data mat lack explanatory variables and other external factors, such as policy change or economic factors so it is not a for sure shot of predicting high accuracy. There may also be low counts of certain data points.
